# FRCP 12(b)(6) Standards — SOL-Based Motion to Dismiss
## Third Circuit / Pennsylvania Federal Districts

---

## I. The Twombly/Iqbal Pleading Standard

**Bell Atlantic Corp. v. Twombly, 550 U.S. 544 (2007):**
A complaint must allege "enough facts to state a claim to relief that is plausible on its face." More than "labels and conclusions" or "a formulaic recitation of the elements" is required. Factual allegations must "raise a right to relief above the speculative level."

**Ashcroft v. Iqbal, 556 U.S. 662 (2009):**
Two-step framework:
1. Identify legal elements of the claim; strip conclusory allegations
2. Assess whether remaining factual allegations plausibly state a claim

**Phillips v. County of Allegheny, 515 F.3d 224, 231 (3d Cir. 2008):**
"The court must accept as true all of the factual allegations in the complaint, and draw all reasonable inferences in favor of the plaintiff."

---

## II. SOL as a 12(b)(6) Basis

**Rycoline Products, Inc. v. C & W Unlimited, 109 F.3d 883, 886 (3d Cir. 1997):**
Affirmative defenses (including SOL, res judicata, statute of frauds) may be raised via 12(b)(6) only when the pleaded facts **conclusively establish the defense** without reference to matters outside the complaint.

**Robinson v. Johnson, 313 F.3d 128, 135 (3d Cir. 2002):**
SOL defense proper at 12(b)(6) stage only when "the time alleged in the statement of the claim shows that the cause of action has not been brought within the statute of limitations." Defendant bears the burden. Any ambiguity or factual development required defeats the motion.

**Wisniewski v. Fisher, 857 F.3d 152, 157 (3d Cir. 2017):**
Reaffirmed: defense must be "apparent on the face of the complaint." Applied in prisoner civil rights context.

**Key rule:** If the complaint contains dates making the SOL bar unambiguous — injury date, knowledge date, filing date — immediate 12(b)(6) motion is proper. If temporal facts are ambiguous, file the SOL as an affirmative defense in the Answer and pursue summary judgment.

---

## III. Standard of Review Language (for use in brief)

Courts consistently apply:
- "The Court accepts as true all well-pleaded factual allegations"
- "All reasonable inferences are drawn in Plaintiff's favor"
- "The Court may not consider matters extraneous to the pleadings" (*In re Burlington Coat Factory Securities Litigation*, 114 F.3d 1410, 1426 (3d Cir. 1997))
- "Dismissal is warranted only if the law affords no remedy, accepting those facts as true"

---

## IV. Handling Tolling Allegations

- Plaintiff need not "plead around" a potential SOL defense
- If plaintiff pleads tolling allegations, court accepts them as true at 12(b)(6)
- However, tolling must be plausibly pleaded — bare assertions insufficient under Twombly/Iqbal
- If tolling requires factual inquiry beyond the complaint, motion should be denied; SOL becomes a summary judgment issue

**Cunningham v. M&T Bank Corp. (3d Cir. 2016):** Plaintiffs must plausibly allege facts supporting equitable tolling; even defendant misconduct does not toll if plaintiff lacked reasonable diligence.

---

## V. Dismissal With Prejudice vs. Leave to Amend

**With prejudice is appropriate when:**
- SOL bar is facially conclusive from the complaint's dates
- No amendment could cure the defect (relation-back under Rule 15(c) typically doesn't apply to originally time-barred claims)
- "Leave to amend would be futile" because passage of time cannot be remedied

**Leave to amend (denial without prejudice) appropriate when:**
- Tolling or accrual questions remain open
- Plaintiff may be able to allege facts supporting tolling
- The SOL bar is not fully resolved from face of complaint

---

## VI. 12(b)(6) vs. Affirmative Defense Strategy

| Scenario | Strategy |
|---|---|
| SOL bar mathematically clear from complaint's own dates | File 12(b)(6) immediately |
| Complaint dates are ambiguous or tolling is plausibly alleged | Plead SOL as affirmative defense in Answer → summary judgment |
| Partial bar (some claims time-barred, some not) | 12(b)(6) as to time-barred claims; Answer as to live claims |
