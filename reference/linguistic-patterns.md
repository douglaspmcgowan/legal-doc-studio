# Legal Linguistic Patterns — Federal Motion to Dismiss
## PA Federal Districts (E.D. Pa., M.D. Pa., W.D. Pa.) / 3rd Circuit

---

## I. Document Structure & Order

1. **Caption** — Court name, case number, parties, judge assignment
2. **Motion title** — e.g., "DEFENDANT'S MOTION TO DISMISS PLAINTIFF'S COMPLAINT PURSUANT TO FEDERAL RULE OF CIVIL PROCEDURE 12(b)(6)"
3. **Motion body** (short, ~1 page) — states grounds, incorporates supporting brief by reference
4. **Supporting Brief/Memorandum of Law** (substantive argument)
   - Introduction / Preliminary Statement
   - Background / Factual Background
   - Legal Standard
   - Argument (Roman numerals + letter/number sub-headings)
   - Conclusion
5. **Certificate of Service**

## III. Argument Heading Style

Roman numerals for main points; capitals for sub-points:

```
I.  PLAINTIFF'S CLAIMS ARE BARRED BY THE APPLICABLE STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS

    A.  Pennsylvania's Two-Year Limitations Period Governs Plaintiff's Claims

    B.  The Complaint Establishes That Plaintiff's Claims Accrued More Than
        Two Years Before This Action Was Filed

    C.  No Tolling Doctrine Saves Plaintiff's Time-Barred Claims
```

Main headings: ALL CAPS or title case (varies by judge preference — check standing orders). Sub-headings: title case.

## IV. Precise SOL Argument Phrases

**Opening the SOL argument:**
- "Plaintiff's claims are time-barred and must be dismissed."
- "On the face of the Complaint, it is apparent that Plaintiff's claims were not filed within the applicable statute of limitations."
- "The Complaint's own allegations establish that this action was filed [X years] after Plaintiff's claims accrued."

**Establishing accrual:**
- "A cause of action accrues under Pennsylvania law when the plaintiff has a right to institute suit."
- "Plaintiff's claims accrued on or about [date], as the Complaint itself alleges."
- "At paragraph [X] of the Complaint, Plaintiff alleges that [triggering event]. This event established Plaintiff's right to sue no later than [date]."

**Showing the bar:**
- "Plaintiff filed this action on [date]. Under the applicable [X]-year statute of limitations, Plaintiff was required to file by [date]. This action was filed [X days/months/years] too late."
- "Because Plaintiff's claims accrued more than [X years] before this action was commenced, they are time-barred under 42 Pa.C.S. § [section]."

**Addressing tolling:**
- "Plaintiff has not alleged any facts that would support tolling of the statute of limitations."
- "Bare assertions of equitable tolling are insufficient under the pleading standard established in Twombly and Iqbal."
- "Even accepting Plaintiff's allegations as true, the discovery rule does not save Plaintiff's claims. The Complaint establishes that Plaintiff [knew / had constructive notice] of [injury/causation] as of [date]."
- "Pennsylvania's discovery rule is 'employed only for worthy cases' and 'cannot be applied so loosely as to nullify the purpose for which a statute of limitations exists.'" (citing *Dalrymple v. Brown*, 701 A.2d 164, 167 (Pa. 1997))

**Futility of amendment:**
- "Leave to amend would be futile. The passage of time cannot be cured by amendment, and no re-pleading could render Plaintiff's claims timely."
- "Because the time-bar is apparent on the face of the Complaint, dismissal with prejudice is warranted."

## V. Standard of Review Boilerplate

```
Under Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 12(b)(6), a court must dismiss
a complaint that "fail[s] to state a claim upon which relief can be
granted." Fed. R. Civ. P. 12(b)(6). To survive a motion to dismiss,
a complaint must contain "sufficient factual matter, accepted as true,
to state a claim to relief that is plausible on its face." Ashcroft
v. Iqbal, 556 U.S. 662, 678 (2009) (quoting Bell Atl. Corp. v.
Twombly, 550 U.S. 544, 570 (2007)). The Court accepts all well-pleaded
factual allegations as true and draws all reasonable inferences in the
plaintiff's favor. Phillips v. Cty. of Allegheny, 515 F.3d 224, 231
(3d Cir. 2008). An affirmative defense such as the statute of
limitations may be raised in a 12(b)(6) motion where the defense is
apparent on the face of the complaint. Robinson v. Johnson, 313 F.3d
128, 135 (3d Cir. 2002).
```

## VI. Tone and Register

- **Professional-aggressive:** "The Complaint cannot survive scrutiny." / "There is no plausible reading of the Complaint that places Plaintiff's claims within the limitations period."
- **Objective framing:** Ground everything in the complaint's own allegations. "As alleged in paragraph [X]…" / "Plaintiff's own Complaint reveals…"
- **Avoid:** Inflammatory characterizations of opposing counsel; hyperbole; passive constructions where active is clearer.
- **Conclusion register:** "For the foregoing reasons, Defendant respectfully requests that the Court dismiss Plaintiff's Complaint in its entirety, with prejudice, pursuant to Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 12(b)(6)."
